Concepts and terminology
Foreign exchange

Foreign exchange transactions are the exchange of one foreign currency into another, that is, to buy one currency in a currency portfolio and sell another currency at the same time. The foreign exchange market has no specific location, no central exchange, but through electronic networks among banks, enterprises and individuals to carry out currency transactions. The exchange rates of various currencies in the international market fluctuate frequently and trade in the form of currency pairs, such as the euro/dollar or the dollar/yen.

Differential contract

A spread contract can reflect the price change of a commodity or certificate and provide the profit or loss caused by the price change without actually owning the commodity or certificate itself.

20 noteworthy economic data in foreign exchange transactions

The economic data of various countries mainly influence the foreign exchange market from the psychological level. These data mainly include GDP, producer price index, consumer price index, retail price index and unemployment rate. As a foreign exchange investor, in addition to focusing on these data, you also need to learn how to analyze their impact on the market

Let's take a look at 20 economic data that are important to the foreign exchange market and their importance, causes, interpretations, and frequency of release.

Gross Domestic Product as GDP）

GDP refers to the value of all the final products and services produced in the economy of a country or region within a certain period of time (a quarter or a year), and is often recognized as the best indicator of the state of the economy. It can not only reflect the economic performance of a country, but also reflect the national strength and wealth of a country.

Importance Very high, especially when compared with market expectations, the actual release of economic growth or recession rates often dictates the direction of financial markets. The higher the data, the stronger the economic growth

Why (why do investors care?) A country's GDP has increased substantially, reflecting the country's booming economic development, increased national income and increased consumption capacity. In this case, the central bank will be likely to raise interest rates, tighten the money supply, the country's good economic performance and the rise in interest rates will increase the attractiveness of the currency. Conversely, if a country's GDP grows negatively, it shows that the country's economy is in a recession and consumption capacity is reduced. At this point, the central bank may cut interest rates to stimulate the economy to grow again, and the attraction of the country's currency will diminish as interest rates fall and economic performance weakens. Therefore, generally speaking, high economic growth rate will drive up the exchange rate of the domestic currency, while low economic growth rate will cause the exchange rate of the national currency to fall

Interpretation GDP represents all economic activities within a country, reflects the basic situation of economic growth, and is used to analyze the current state of economic development. GDP growth rate accelerated, indicating that the economy is in the expansion stage, the consumption demand for production materials will increase. Usually the higher the GDP in the US means the better the economic development, the higher interest rate and the stronger dollar exchange rate

frequency Every quarter and every year, and every time the data will be corrected two times

Consumer price index (CPI) Consumer price index (CPI), also known as consumer price index (CPI), is a measure of the price of a fixed basket of consumer goods. It mainly reflects the price changes of goods and services paid by consumers. It is also a tool to measure the level of inflation in the form of percentage changes. It is also a price change indicator used to measure the prices of products and services related to the lives of residents.The major commodities that make up the index in the United States fall into seven categories, including food, alcohol and beverages, housing, clothing, transportation, medical health, entertainment, and other goods and services. In the United States, the CPI is published monthly by the Bureau of Labor Statistics. There are two different CPIs. The first is the CPI of workers and staff, or CPW. The two is the consumer price index of urban consumers, or CPIU. Importance High usually affects financial markets, and its timeliness and inflationary implications are widely tracked Why (why do investors care?) Consumer price indices are important and revelatory and must be handled with caution, as they are sometimes announced to rise and currencies to exchange rates to the contrary. Because the level of CPI indicates the purchasing power of consumers, but also reflects the economic situation, if the index falls, reflecting the economic recession, is bound to be unfavorable to the currency exchange rate trend. But if the consumer price index rises, is the exchange rate necessarily favorable? Not necessarily, it depends on the "rise" of the consumer price index. If the index rises moderately, it indicates that the economy is stable and upward, which is beneficial to the currency of the country, but if the index rises too much, it will have a negative impact because the price index is inversely proportional to the purchasing power. The more expensive the price index is, the lower the purchasing power of the currency is, which is bound to be detrimental to the currency of the country. If the interest rate is considered, the impact of the index on foreign exchange rate will be more complicated. frequency When a country's CPI rises, it indicates that its inflation rate rises, that is, the purchasing power of its currency weakens. According to the theory of purchasing power parity, its currency should weaken. On the contrary, when a country's CPI declines, it indicates that the country's inflation rate drops, that is, the purchasing power of the currency rises. According to PPP theory, the country's currency should strengthen. However, since all countries have to control inflation as the primary task, rising inflation also brings the opportunity for interest rates to rise, so on the contrary, favor the currency. If inflation is controlled and falls and interest rates tend to fall at the same time, it will weaken the currency of the region. The Consumer Confidence Index of the Consultative Council represents that consumers have a strong desire to consume goods and services, which is conducive to economic expansion. Bond market investors prefer a downward decline in consumer confidence index, because it represents a weak willingness to consume, the possibility of economic slowdown increases. Usually the dollar looks to the Fed for hints that if consumer confidence rises, that means consumer growth, a stronger economy, and the Fed may raise interest rates, then the dollar strengthens accordingly. Interpretation Consumer confidence index is mainly to understand the degree of consumer confidence in the economic environment, reflecting consumer views on the economy and purchase intention. The report includes both consumer assessments of the economy and the job market, as well as expectations of the future economy and the job market, as well as questions about household income and plans to buy consumer goods such as homes and cars. frequency monthly
